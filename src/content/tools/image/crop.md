# Free Online Image Cropper

Welcome to the most secure, privacy-first image cropping tool on the web. Whether you are prepping an image for social media, cutting out unwanted background elements, or just refining your composition, File Forge's Image Cropper delivers a professional-grade experience directly in your browser.

## Why Crop Locally?

Traditional online image editors force you to upload your personal or professional photos to a remote server. This exposes your data to privacy risks, requires a fast internet connection, and forces you to wait in a server queue.

File Forge is different. Our architecture is built entirely on WebAssembly and modern browser APIs. This means:

- **Absolute Privacy**: Your image is loaded entirely into your local machine's memory. It never touches our servers.
- **Zero Latency**: No waiting for uploads or downloads. Adjust your crop box and see the results instantly.
- **No File Size Limits**: Because you aren't uploading anything over the network, you can process massive ultra-high-resolution images without arbitrary server caps.
- **Offline Capable**: Once the app is loaded, you can crop images even if you disconnect from the internet.

## Professional Crop Features

Our image cropper isn't just a basic tool. It is packed with features designed for creators, designers, and everyday users who demand precision:

### 1. Aspect Ratio Constraints

Quickly snap your crop area to popular aspect ratios to ensure your image fits perfectly on any platform:

- **1:1 (Square)**: Perfect for Instagram posts and profile pictures.
- **16:9 (Landscape)**: Ideal for YouTube thumbnails, presentations, and desktop wallpapers.
- **4:3 (Landscape)**: The standard ratio for digital photography.
- **9:16 (Portrait)**: Perfect for TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.
- **Original Image**: Maintain the exact original aspect ratio of your photo while shrinking the frame.

### 2. Aspect Ratio Lock

If you have a custom dimension in mind but want to scale it up or down seamlessly, you can lock the aspect ratio. When adjusting the width or height sliders, the other dimension will automatically scale to keep the image in perfect proportion without distortion.

### 3. Precision Sliders & Inputs

Move beyond clunky mouse dragging. Use our professional offset and sizing sliders to move your crop box pixel-by-pixel. If you know exactly how many pixels wide or tall your image needs to be, simply type it into the width and height inputs.

### 4. Non-Destructive Editing

The crop is purely visual until you are ready to export. You can adjust the crop constraints, move the box around, scale it, or hit the **Reset** button to start over completely. The original image data is always preserved in memory.

## How to Crop an Image

1. **Select Your Image**: Drag and drop your JPG, PNG, WebP, or AVIF file into the workspace, or click to upload.
2. **Open Crop Settings**: In the right-hand Properties Panel, select the "Crop & Size" section.
3. **Set Constraints (Optional)**: Choose a predefined aspect ratio from the dropdown, or stick to "Free" for a custom shape.
4. **Adjust the Box**: Drag the crop handles on the visual canvas, or use the precision sliders in the panel to define your exact bounds.
5. **Export**: Click the "Export" button in the top bar. You can choose to export your cropped image as a PNG (for lossless quality), JPEG, or WebP.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does cropping reduce image quality?**
No! Cropping simply removes the outer pixels of an image. The pixels that remain inside your crop box are completely untouched and retain their original 100% resolution.

**Is this safe for sensitive photos?**
Absolutely. Because File Forge operates entirely client-side, your images never leave your device. There are no servers, no databases, and no cloud storage involved in the editing process. It is mathematically impossible for us to see or store your files.

**Can I crop multiple images at once?**
Currently, our workspace is optimized for single-canvas editing for maximum precision. However, you can drag multiple images into the workspace as different layers and arrange them before cropping the master canvas!

**Why does the file size change when I export?**
When you export an image, our engine re-encodes the remaining pixels into a new file format. Depending on the format you choose (JPEG vs PNG) and the quality settings you apply, the file size will change. We provide a live preview of the final file size before you download it.
